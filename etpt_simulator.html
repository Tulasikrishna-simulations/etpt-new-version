THE PROPOSITION
Blind humans share B with sighted humans but have a rotated P — visual columns zeroed, tactile column elevated through cross-modal cortical reassignment. The largest cell of the human B⊗P matrix is (fine motor × tactile) at 0.833 — and in blind humans, this cell expands further to 0.931. Build haptic hardware that crosses ρ_c, place it on cortex that has been waiting to recruit, and the result is not vision restored: it is a new perceptual category the world has not had a word for. The blind community's cortex is uniquely ready. This simulator shows the mechanism live.

The matrix rotation · sighted human → blind human

Same biological vector B; perception vector P rotated by cross-modal cortical reassignment. Sadato (1996), Pascual-Leone (2001), Amedi (2003) document the rewiring; ETPT specifies its structure. The visual columns (vis_EM, FOV) close; the tactile column (P₄) expands. ‖M‖ ≈ 1.86 → 1.95.

Phenomenology · what the subject experiences right now

The percept is not vision. It is a new perceptual category that emerges as ρ_eff crosses ρ_c and P(t) grows. Daniel Kish (flash sonar) and Bach-y-Rita's TVSS users describe similar transitions in their own words. The vocabulary doesn't exist yet.
Stage 1 · subthreshold
"It buzzes. It tickles. I feel the device on my skin but it doesn't mean anything beyond that."
Stage 2 · threshold crossing
"Something is out there. I can't quite name it. Patterns I feel on my back point toward… the room?"
Stage 3 · spatial emergence
"The chair is to my left. There's a wall ahead, three meters. I'm not feeling the device anymore — I'm feeling the room."
Stage 4 · the new sense
"I don't know what to call it. It isn't sight. It isn't touch. The world is just there, in a way that nobody has a word for yet."

Four predictions of blind-native technology · already converging in the real world

The framework predicts that blind users with design freedom converge on four technology paradigms matching their elevated P. Each prediction has documented evidence from the existing blind community — not from labs, not from researchers, but from the community designing for itself.
01
Dynamic topological interfaces
Computing surfaces that change shape and texture, not flat screens. The information lives in the deformation of the surface itself.
Evidence in the world BLITAB (2017, 8000-pin tactile tablet), Graphiti display, Orbit Reader, Optacon (1971). When blind users have design budget, they converge here.
02
Resonance architecture
Buildings and urban infrastructure encode wayfinding through texture and acoustics. Space is felt and heard, not seen.
Evidence in the world Tenji blocks (Seiichi Miyake, 1965 — now international standard). The Lighthouse for the Blind, SF. APS pedestrian signals.
03
Tele-haptics
Communication directly through touch over distance. Felt emotion and meaning rather than mediated by text or visual icons.
Evidence in the world Apple Watch haptic heartbeats (mass-market validation). Neosensory Buzz. Purdue HIRL vibrotactile messaging.
04
Spatial audio matrices
3D positioned sound as the dominant interface for environmental information. Convergent independent development by blind users themselves.
Evidence in the world Microsoft Soundscape (designed with blind users). BlindSquare. Daniel Kish's flash sonar — independent reinvention.

Live equations

Tpath = (B ⊗ P) · D · (1 − e−αS)
Mirror Rule. Technology direction is determined by the bio-perception tensor; intensity by density and pressure. Direction and intensity decouple.
dP/dt = β · (ρ − ρc)+ · (Pmax − P) · f
Equation 1. Cortical reassignment dynamics. The rectified (·)₊ term implements the subthreshold lock: below ρ_c, dP/dt = 0 and P never grows.
ρc = Cv / [A · R · log₂(1 + SNR)]
Equation 2. Reclassification threshold derived from Shannon channel capacity. Central estimate ρ_c ≈ 10⁵ bits/cm²/s for typical haptic-cortical parameters.
Q = σ((ρeff − ρc)/ρc) · P(t) · f
Derived perceptual quality. Combines threshold proximity, current recruitment, and population plasticity into a single spatial-percept probability metric.
