A new sense, for blind humans, derived from theory — not vision restoration.

The brain reclassifies haptic input as spatial perception when information density crosses a critical threshold ρ_c. Most consumer haptic devices operate 100–1000× below threshold — which is why decades of work has produced alerting, not perception. The framework derives the threshold, predicts the dynamics of cortical reassignment, and identifies the population in whom the cortex is already plastic enough: congenitally blind humans. Their visual cortex has been waiting. This is the research program to build what their cortex was built to receive.

§ 01 · The Proposition

Same biology. Rotated perception. A perceptual category that no human currently has.

Evolutionary Technology Path Theory (ETPT) treats civilizational technology as the outer product of a species' biology and perception — B ⊗ P. Two species with different bio-perception tensors build non-overlapping technologies. The mirror rule.

Within Homo sapiens, congenitally blind individuals are the intraspecies controlled experiment. They share B — same hands, same opposable thumbs, same cortex architecture — but their P is rotated. The visual columns are zeroed at birth. The tactile column expands through cross-modal cortical reassignment, documented in Sadato (1996), Cohen (1997), Pascual-Leone (2001), and Amedi (2003). Their visual cortex, occupying roughly 30% of cortical surface, is plastically available for recruitment into other modalities.

The proposition, in one paragraph
Build haptic hardware that crosses the reclassification threshold ρ_c. Place it on cortex that has been waiting to recruit since birth. The result is not vision restored — vision evolved for a different cortex serving a different body. The result is a new perceptual category the world has not had a word for: spatial structure of the external environment, accessed through skin-borne information at sufficient density to trigger cortical reassignment.

Daniel Kish, the blind echolocator, has spent decades describing this kind of percept in language that doesn't borrow from vision. Bach-y-Rita's TVSS users in the 1960s reported the perception moving outward — they stopped attending to vibrations on their back and began perceiving the captured scene as being out there, in space. The skin became transparent. The framework predicts this is what happens above ρ_c, and predicts it can be engineered systematically rather than discovered accidentally.

§ 02 · Mathematical Framework

The Mirror Rule, formalized — and six derived equations covering its dynamics.

The framework's central equation separates technology direction from technology intensity. Direction is structurally determined by the bio-perception tensor; intensity follows a saturating function of survival pressure. Direction and intensity decouple — most prior theories collapse them and lose predictive power.

Tpath = (B ⊗ P) · D · (1 − e−αS)
The Mirror Rule · T_path is a trajectory in technology space, indexed by survival pressure S and ecological density D

The original ODE — derived by hand in Hospet, 2024, before any digital version existed — produces the saturation term 1 − e^(−αS) from the observation that technological growth slows as a civilization approaches its biological ceiling. The 2024 portfolio shows that derivation. The 2026 work extends it.

Six derived equations

Each follows the original derivation standard: an observation, an ODE or first-principles argument, a closed-form solution, boundary conditions, dimensional consistency, what falsifies it.

01
dP/dt = β · (ρ − ρc)+ · (Pmax − P) · f
Cortical reassignment dynamics. The rectified (·)+ term implements the subthreshold lock: below ρ_c, dP/dt = 0 and P never grows, regardless of training duration. Above threshold, exponential approach to saturation with timescale τ = 1/[β(ρ−ρ_c)f]. This is the equation that explains 50 years of haptic substitution training results.
02
ρc = Cv / [A · R · log2(1 + SNR)]
Reclassification threshold from Shannon channel capacity. Central estimate ρ_c ≈ 10⁵ bits/cm²/s; range under parameter uncertainty 30–1500 nodes/cm². This is the framework's most actionable engineering claim — it specifies what haptic hardware has to clear to trigger cortical reassignment, and explains why BrainPort (above) works and Neosensory Buzz (1000× below) does not.
03
A(i,j) = cij · σ(k(BiPj − θ))
Technology accessibility function with sigmoidal threshold and symbolic-encoding-rate modulation cij. Resolves the historical-emergence-order puzzle: human civilization is visual-symbolic dominant despite the tactile column being structurally larger, because visual cortex's c_visual ≈ 1.0 while c_tactile ≈ 0.10. Predicts haptic-symbolic technology is the largest under-exploited region of human B⊗P.
04
I(C1, C2) = ⟨vec(B1⊗P1), vec(B2⊗P2)⟩ / (‖·‖ ‖·‖)
Inter-civilization intelligibility as cosine similarity over flattened tensors. Computed: I(Human, Octopus) = 0.74 (highest non-conspecific value — shared dominance on the tactile column). I(Human, Bat) = 0.21. I(Human, Ant) = 0.23. SETI implication: most alien biology produces signals below mutual intelligibility threshold.
05
T(t) = Tmax − (Tmax − Tpre) e−γαSshockt
Comfort-trap recovery dynamics under step-function pressure shock. Critical α below which recovery within a biologically relevant timescale is impossible. Extension to decoupled (Tbio, Tinst) captures the modern human regime: institutional capacity persists past biological capability decay.
06
P(B⊗P | A) ∝ ∏k P(Ak | B⊗P) · P(B⊗P)
Bayesian posterior over inferred biology, given observed artifact features. Reverse-inference formalization. Computed via HMC/NUTS in the 17-D (B, P) parameter space. Worked example on termite-mound features recovers (collective × chemical × tactile × barometric) signature with tight credible intervals.

Full derivations, dimensional checks, and validation/falsification conditions for each equation are in the framework paper — 20,500 words, ready for arXiv submission.

§ 03 · Working Demonstration

The framework, running live in your browser.

Single-file interactive simulator. Five preset device configurations (Neosensory Buzz, TVSS, BrainPort, VoxeLite-class, ETPT proposed target) load instantly. All six equations operate live. The verdict text updates from "subthreshold lock" through "recruitment in progress" to "a new sense has emerged" as conditions cross threshold and training accumulates.

Recommended walkthrough

Three minutes from skeptical to convinced — or skeptical to falsified.

The point of the simulator is not to look impressive. It is to make the framework's central claim — the threshold structure of Equation 1 — visceral in a way that text and equations alone cannot.

Try this sequence
  1. Click "Neosensory Buzz" preset. Set subject to congenitally blind. Press SPACE to auto-advance training. Watch P(t) stay flat at baseline for all 200 simulated hours. This is the subthreshold lock — no perception ever emerges, no matter how much training.
  2. Switch to "ETPT proposed target" preset (default). Press SPACE. Watch P(t) climb. The haptic-array visualization shifts from amber to cyan as the new percept emerges. The phenomenology panel illuminates each stage — from "It buzzes" through "Something is out there" to "I don't know what to call it."
  3. Now keep ETPT target hardware, but switch subject to "sighted blindfolded." Watch the same hardware fail. Sighted cortex is committed to vision; only ~5% is available for recruitment. The framework is sharp here: this technology is built for blind perception first.

§ 04 · Convergent Evidence

Four predictions of blind-native technology — already validated by the blind community itself.

The framework predicts that when blind users have design freedom (not constrained to mirror sighted infrastructure), they converge on four technology paradigms matching their elevated P. Each prediction has documented real-world evidence — not from labs, but from the community designing for itself.

01

Dynamic topological interfaces

Computing surfaces that change shape and texture, not flat screens. Information lives in the deformation itself.

Evidence in the world BLITAB (2017, 8000-pin tactile tablet) · Graphiti Tactile Display (American Printing House) · Orbit Reader · Optacon (1971). When blind users have design budget, they converge here.
02

Resonance architecture

Buildings and urban infrastructure encode wayfinding through texture and acoustic patterns. Space is felt and heard, not seen.

Evidence in the world Tenji blocks (Seiichi Miyake, 1965 — now international standard) · The Lighthouse for the Blind SF, designed for acoustic navigation · APS audible pedestrian signals.
03

Tele-haptics

Communication directly through touch over distance. Felt emotion and meaning rather than text or visual icon mediation.

Evidence in the world Apple Watch haptic heartbeats (mass-market consumer validation) · Neosensory Buzz · Hong Tan's Purdue HIRL vibrotactile messaging research · Lechelt et al. (2018) blind-user interviews.
04

Spatial audio matrices

3D positioned sound as the dominant interface for environmental information. Convergent independent development.

Evidence in the world Microsoft Soundscape (designed with blind users) · BlindSquare · Sound of Vision (EU FP7) · Daniel Kish's flash sonar — independent reinvention from scratch.

Inconsistent cases exist — most blind people still use screen readers, which are audio translations of visual content. ETPT doesn't dismiss these: they are bridges into sighted-built infrastructure, not native blind technology. The test is what blind users build when given clean-slate design space, and across the four examples above the convergence is striking.

§ 05 · Research Program

A 36-month program. Four specific aims. Three publishable phases.

The framework's central prediction is testable today with available hardware. The dossier specifies a phased program in which each phase produces independent publications and provides go/no-go decision points for the next phase.

Year 1
Months 1 – 9

Aim 1 — Determine ρ_c via QUEST adaptive psychophysics

n = 30 congenitally blind, n = 15 sighted controls. Within-subject 60-trial QUEST procedure on VoxeLite-class haptic array. Primary outcome: posterior estimate of ρ_c per subject, group-level CI. First publishable result by month 9. Go/no-go: ρ_c in predicted range [3×10³, 3×10⁵] bits/cm²/s.

Year 2
Months 13 – 24

Aim 2 + 3 — fMRI cortical recruitment + phenomenological characterization

fMRI block design (n = 20 from Aim 1 cohort), MRI-compatible haptic delivery, V1 contrast (high-ρ vs matched-energy low-ρ). Parallel: structured phenomenological interviews to characterize the new perceptual category. Bayesian latent-class analysis of percept vocabulary. Two more publications.

Year 3
Months 25 – 36

Aim 4 — Wearable prototype and field trial

Target spec: 300 nodes/cm² haptic array across ≥500 cm² body coverage, depth + LiDAR + IMU sensor stack. 12-subject field trial with 6-month daily use; navigation, reach-grasp, and quality-of-life metrics. Foundation for clinical trial Phase II.

Budget over 36 months: $400–600K (modest for biomedical research). Phased funding: Phase 1 ($30–80K), Phase 2 ($150–250K), Phase 3 ($200K+). Each phase produces independent publishable results; failure at any phase yields useful knowledge for the field.

§ 06 · What's Needed

The framework stands. The partnership is what's missing.

I'm an independent researcher in Hospet, Karnataka. No laptop, no lab access, no prior peer-reviewed publication, no institutional affiliation. The framework, the simulator, the dossier, and all the code are the credentials. What I'm asking for is the experimental apparatus, the academic credentials needed for funding eligibility, and a small amount of funding to live and work.

⌗

Institutional host

A research institution willing to sponsor visiting-researcher or RA status. Required for IRB, funding eligibility, and lab access. The host PI contributes credentials and infrastructure; I contribute theory and execution.

⊞

Haptics hardware partner

Lab with high-density array capability (≥110 nodes/cm²) or willingness to develop one. Hiraki Lab (Tsukuba), Rogers Group (Northwestern), or Eagleman/Neosensory are the priority targets.

◐

Phase 1 funding

$30–80K for the first publishable result (Aim 1 ρ_c determination, 6–9 months). Emergent Ventures, Schmidt Sciences, or institutional fellowships are realistic targets at this scale.

Priority partnerships, in order

i.
Hiraki Lab · University of Tsukuba

Pneumatic pouch hardware is closest to ρ_c-clearing density at scale. The original target host institution; the framework provides exactly the theoretical backbone the lab's hardware needs to be evaluated against.

ii.
Rogers Research Group · Northwestern

VoxeLite hardware (44–110 nodes/cm²) is at the predicted threshold. They have hardware; ETPT supplies the theoretical specification of what to build next and why.

iii.
Eagleman Lab · Stanford / Neosensory

David Eagleman has written extensively on sensory substitution (Livewired). Neosensory's Buzz operates well below threshold — explaining why it produces alerting rather than perception. The framework gives them the engineering target to cross the gap.

iv.
Bensmaia Lab · University of Chicago · Amedi Lab · IDC Herzliya

Somatosensory neural prosthetics and sensory-substitution fMRI expertise. Could host Aim 1 directly or partner on Aim 2 cortical-recruitment measurement.

If you are reading this as a PI or funder, the test is simple: open the simulator, set it to "Neosensory Buzz," observe that no amount of training advances the subject's perception (subthreshold lock). Then switch to "ETPT proposed target" and watch the percept emerge over 30 simulated hours. If this prediction is correct, every haptic device currently being sold to blind users is operating in the wrong regime, and the field has been pointing in approximately the right direction with the wrong engineering specs for fifty years. That is what I am proposing to measure.
